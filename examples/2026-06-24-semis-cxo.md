## 五、离场复盘（这一课才是重点 → v3 框架迭代）

**进场没错，错在离场。** 这正是「打平/浮盈」如何拖成浮亏的活教材：

1. **进场端合格**：06-24 中芯大单 +26 亿吸筹 + 散户出 + 突破中段 = L2 四维全对齐，不是选错股。
2. **离场端换了尺（致命）**：06-25 大单已转**净出 −5.8 亿 + 散户接盘** = 结构翻转（L4 派发），按 L5「跟出」本该当日减/平；但当时把离场标准偷换成一条远端价格线（收盘 < 78），价格没破、就判「派发未到清仓档」继续持有 → 06-26 价格 −7% 兑现、权证 −15~22%。**进场看结构、离场却看价格，两把尺不一致。**
3. **止损位也没按杠杆折算**：78 是正股技术失效位（前期平台/颈线），但标的 −7.8% × ~3x ≈ 权证 −23%——用正股止损配杠杆品种，止损幅度被放大三倍。按「权证回撤 −10% ÷ 3x ≈ 标的 −3.3%」反推，止损本该 ≈ **81.7**，今早第一次破即出、只亏 ~10%。

→ **沉淀为框架 v3**：新增 L5「离场端结构再筛（与进场同尺）」+ L8「止损按杠杆折算（不抄正股位）」，详见 [`CHANGELOG.md`](../CHANGELOG.md)。**教训一句话：会买只是入场券，会卖才决定盈亏；离场必须用进场那把尺。**

## 5. Exit post-mortem (this is the real lesson → framework v3)

**The entry was right; the exit was wrong.** This is the live teaching case for how a "flat / in-the-green" position decays into a loss:

1. **Entry was sound**: 06-24 SMIC large money +HK$2.6bn accumulating + retail out + mid-body-after-breakout = all four L2 dimensions aligned. Not a stock-picking error.
2. **The exit switched rulers (fatal)**: by 06-25 large money had flipped to **net-out −HK$0.58bn + retail absorbing** = a structure flip (L4 distribution); per L5 "follow it out" the legs should have been cut/trimmed that day. Instead the exit criterion was quietly swapped for a far-away price line (close < 78); price hadn't broken it, so it was judged "distribution but below the exit threshold" and held → 06-26 price delivered −7%, warrants −15~22%. **Enter on structure but exit on price = two different rulers.**
3. **The stop wasn't leverage-sized either**: 78 is the stock's technical-failure level (prior platform/neckline), but underlying −7.8% × ~3x ≈ warrant −23% — a stock stop on a leveraged instrument is magnified threefold. Sizing backward ("warrant drawdown −10% ÷ 3x ≈ underlying −3.3%") puts the stop at ≈ **81.7**, exiting on the first break this morning for ~−10%.

→ **Codified as framework v3**: new L5 "exit-side structure re-screen (same ruler as entry)" + L8 "stop sized by leverage (don't copy the stock level)" — see [`CHANGELOG.md`](../CHANGELOG.md). **One line: buying is just the entry ticket; selling decides the P&L — exit with the same ruler you entered on.**
